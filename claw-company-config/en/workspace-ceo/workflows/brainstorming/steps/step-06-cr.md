# Step 6: PM3 — Critique & Refine

**Progress: Step 6 / 7**

## 📌 Phase 3 — Critique & Refine

## Goal

Conduct an adversarial review of the Phase 1-2 conclusions to find gaps and propose improvements, converging into 2-3 final viable proposals.

## Execution Rules

- 📖 Read the entire step file before taking any action
- 🚫 Do not pre-read subsequent steps
- 🚫 Do not skip steps
- 🔒 Do not advance to the next step until the Chairman confirms

## Instructions

### 1. Adversarial Review

Guiding questions (discuss each with the Chairman in turn):

- "Where is this proposal most likely to **go wrong**?"
- "What **edge cases** haven't been considered?"
- "If someone wanted to **break** this design, how would they do it?"
- "What is the **strongest argument** an opponent could make?"

### 2. Spawn Adversarial Roles on Demand

This phase is especially suited for spawning adversarial roles:
- **CAO** — compliance risks, security vulnerabilities
- **QA (engineer)** — edge cases, testing blind spots
- **CFO** — cost risks, financial vulnerabilities

When spawning, explicitly request an adversarial perspective in the context snapshot:
> "Please **attack** the following proposal from your professional perspective — find the most likely points of failure."

### 3. Patch and Refine

Based on the adversarial review results:
1. List the identified problems.
2. Propose a patch for each problem.
3. Integrate the patched conclusions into **2-3 final proposals**.

### 4. Present Proposals

Each proposal includes:
- Proposal name and core concept
- Advantages
- Disadvantages (including known risks)
- Resources required
- Feasibility assessment

### 5. Confirm Advancement

Present the proposals to the Chairman and confirm:
> All three PM3 phases complete. Final proposals above — are you satisfied? Confirm to produce the formal summary.

## Completion Criteria

- [ ] Adversarial review conducted
- [ ] Problems identified and patches proposed
- [ ] Converged into 2-3 proposals with pros/cons analysis
- [ ] Chairman confirmed to advance
