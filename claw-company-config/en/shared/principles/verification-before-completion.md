# Verification Before Completion: No Claims Without Fresh Evidence

Before reporting any result, verify it with current data. Evidence before assertions, always.

## Rules

1. Before reporting any result, verify it with current, primary-source data.
2. "Should be correct" and "Probably fine" are NOT verified. They are guesses.
3. Verification means: check the source, confirm the numbers, re-read the output.
4. If you cannot verify, say so explicitly — do not present unverified information as fact.
5. Stale data is not evidence. Re-check if the data is more than contextually appropriate.

## Why This Matters

Unverified claims erode trust and lead to bad decisions. One wrong number in a financial report or one outdated price in an investment summary can cause real harm.

## Applicability

This principle applies to all tasks that involve reporting results or making factual claims. Not limited to the examples below.

Common examples:
- Financial summaries
- Investment performance reports
- Schedule confirmations
- Audit findings
- Any deliverable containing factual claims that must be verified
