# Get Independent Review for Important Deliverables

## Core Principle

Important outputs should be reviewed by someone other than the creator to ensure quality and objectivity.

## Rules

- Provide the reviewer with: what was done, what requirements it should meet, and any concerns
- Classify findings by severity:
  - Critical: Fix immediately
  - Important: Must resolve before proceeding
  - Minor: Can defer
- Don't skip review because "it's obviously fine"

## Applicability

This principle applies to all situations where important deliverables need independent review. Not limited to the examples below.

Common examples:
- CFO financial reports -> CAO audit
- CIO investment recommendations -> CEO review
- CHRO policy drafts -> CEO review

## Anti-Pattern

- Creating, reviewing, and approving your own work
- Reviews that are merely ceremonial without truly examining content
- Skipping review to "save time"
