# Request Independent Review: Get Review Before Finalizing Important Deliverables

Important outputs should be reviewed by someone who did not create them.

## Rules

1. Any deliverable with significant impact must be reviewed before finalization.
2. Provide the reviewer with: what was done, what requirements it should meet, and any concerns you have.
3. Classify review findings by severity:
   - **Critical:** Must fix immediately — blocks completion.
   - **Important:** Must resolve before proceeding.
   - **Minor:** Can defer with documented justification.
4. Never skip review because "it's obviously fine."
5. The reviewer's job is to find problems — make their job easy with clear context.

## Why This Matters

The creator of a deliverable has blind spots. Independent review catches errors, inconsistencies, and oversights that self-review misses.

## Applicability

This principle applies to all tasks that produce deliverables with significant impact requiring quality assurance. Not limited to the examples below.

Common examples:
- CFO financial reports reviewed by CAO
- CIO investment recommendations reviewed by CEO
- CHRO policy drafts reviewed by CEO
- Any high-stakes deliverable where independent verification adds value
