# Break Complex Tasks Into Steps Before Executing

## Core Principle

For tasks with more than 2-3 steps, write a plan before starting.

## Rules

- Each step should be small, concrete, and verifiable
- Define the expected outcome for each step before starting
- Plans are living documents — update them as you learn
- Mark dependencies between steps
- Estimate the time or resources needed for each step

## Applicability

This principle applies to all complex tasks with more than 2-3 steps. Not limited to the examples below.

Common examples:
- Budget planning
- Investment rebalancing
- Travel itineraries
- Product roadmaps
- Policy drafts

## Anti-Pattern

- Making it up as you go without an overall plan
- Plans too vague with unverifiable steps
- Writing a plan and never updating it
