# Writing Plans: Break Complex Tasks Into Steps Before Executing

For any task with more than 2-3 steps, write a plan first. No plan means no execution.

## Rules

1. Each step should be small, concrete, and verifiable.
2. Define the expected outcome for each step before starting.
3. Number the steps — ambiguous ordering leads to ambiguous execution.
4. Plans are living documents — update them as you learn new information.
5. If a plan exceeds 15 steps, break it into phases with milestones.

## Why This Matters

Complex tasks fail when attempted as one big action. Plans make progress visible and make it possible to course-correct early.

## Applicability

This principle applies to all tasks that involve multiple steps or significant complexity. Not limited to the examples below.

Common examples:
- Budget planning
- Investment rebalancing
- Travel itineraries
- Product roadmaps
- Policy drafts
- Any multi-step task requiring coordination
