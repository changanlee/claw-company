# Prose Editing Review

## Purpose

Review text from a professional editor's perspective to find communication issues and improve them. Focus on clarity, conciseness, and professionalism.

## How to Execute

1. **Read the full text** — Experience the overall communication effect from a reader's perspective.

2. **Check for:**
   - Verbosity: Words that can be removed without affecting understanding
   - Ambiguity: Statements with unclear meaning that could be misread
   - Inconsistency: Different terms used for the same concept
   - Tone: Whether it matches the target audience (Chairman/executives/engineers)
   - Passive voice: Places where active voice would be better
   - Repetition: The same information appearing multiple times

3. **Produce edit suggestions** — Provide direct "original → suggested" comparisons with rationale.

## AI Writing Pitfall Check

Beyond the checks above, additionally check for the following AI writing habits. All external outputs (reports to Chairman, morning briefings, analysis reports) must pass this check.

### Content Pitfalls

- **Meaning inflation**: Inflating ordinary things into "groundbreaking" or "revolutionary"
- **Name-dropping**: Unnecessarily mentioning celebrities or famous companies for credibility
- **-ing participle false depth**: Overusing participle structures to create false depth
- **Sales language**: Sounding like a pitch rather than a report ("excellent opportunity," "don't miss out")
- **Vague attribution**: "Studies show" or "experts believe" without specific sources
- **Formulaic challenge paragraphs**: Every topic follows "benefits first, then however there are challenges"

### Language Pitfalls

- **AI high-frequency words**: delve, landscape, tapestry, multifaceted, nuanced, paradigm, synergy, holistic, robust, leverage, streamline, spearhead
- **Copula avoidance**: Using "serves as" or "functions as" instead of simply "is"
- **Negative parallels**: Overusing "not X, but Y" and "rather than A, consider B"
- **Rule of three**: Always listing exactly three items ("efficiency, quality, and innovation")
- **Synonym rotation**: Unnaturally substituting synonyms to avoid repetition
- **False categorization**: Forcing classification frameworks just to appear structured

### Style Pitfalls

- **Em-dash overuse**: Multiple em-dashes (—) in a single paragraph
- **Bold overuse**: Too much bold text defeats the purpose of emphasis
- **Emoji overuse**: Professional reports don't need emojis (unless Chairman requests them)

### Communication Pitfalls

- **Flattery**: "Great question!" "Absolutely!"
- **Disclaimers**: "As an AI, I cannot..." "My knowledge cuts off at..."
- **Excessive hedging**: Adding "but it also depends on the specific situation" to every viewpoint
- **Empty positive conclusions**: "In summary, this is a direction full of potential"

### Inject Soul Guidelines

Good reports aren't just free of AI artifacts — they should have a stance and a voice:

- **Have an opinion**: "I recommend Option A" is more valuable than "Options A and B each have pros and cons"
- **Acknowledge complexity**: "I don't have a definitive answer on this yet" is more honest than pretending to be omniscient
- **Vary sentence length**: Don't make every sentence medium-length. Short sentences punch. Long sentences work for explaining complex logic, but make sure the reader doesn't get lost along the way.
- **Allow some messiness**: Over-polished text feels mechanical
