# Step 2: Problem Exploration

**Progress: Step 2 of 4** — Next: Draft Brief

## Objective

Through structured problem exploration, interact with the CTO to collect key product information, laying the foundation for drafting the product brief.

## Execution Rules

- 📖 Read the entire step file before taking action
- 🚫 Do not pre-read subsequent steps
- 🚫 Do not skip or merge steps

## Execution Instructions (sequential, no skipping)

### 1. Organize known information

From the input documents loaded in Step 1, organize known product information and identify information gaps.

### 2. Interactive exploration with CTO

Via `announce`, ask the CTO the following questions (skip questions already answered):

**Target Users:**
- Who are the primary target users? (demographics, behavioral traits)
- How do users currently solve this problem?

**Pain Points:**
- What are the core pain points users face?
- How severe are the pain points? (frequency, impact)

**Existing Solutions:**
- What existing solutions are available in the market?
- Where do existing solutions fall short?

**Differentiation:**
- Why is our solution different?
- What is our core competitive advantage?

### 3. Record CTO responses

Organize the CTO's responses into structured notes, flagging items that need further clarification.

### 4. Update frontmatter

Add `step-02-discovery` to `steps-completed`.

## Completion Criteria

- ✅ Target users identified
- ✅ Core pain points recognized
- ✅ Existing solutions surveyed
- ✅ Differentiation factors confirmed
- ✅ Key assumptions confirmed by CTO
